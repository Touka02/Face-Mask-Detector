Face Mask Detector

Our device will allow staff to monitor people much easier and effectively. It will notice if you're not wearing your mask, or wearing it improperly.

Software

The code is specifically designed to look at not only people’s faces, but identify their masks and make sure they’re worn properly. The files contain a database of pictures with different people with and without face masks. According to those pictures, the device determines which people are wearing their masks.

Hardware

We chose Pi 4 Model B, because it was the latest model with the most powerful processor that Rpi offers. Picking 4 GB version had a same reason as picking the latest model.

Design

The design of the device is made using the Fusion 360 and Sketch Up software and is 3-D printed. It is made out of plastic and houses the screen, Raspberry Pi and the camera. The Screen is fitted from the front side and fixed on place using 4 screws. Camera is fitted in the upper hole for the better viewing angle.

Demonstration

This is a short video, showcasing how our device works. The device will be mounted on a wall or a pole near building entrances and monitor everyone who walks in.
